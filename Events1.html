Chem e wood

Previous Next

We organised a Chem-E-Wood Technical and Fun Event for our fellow students. Chem-E-Wood is similar to the BOLLYWOOD game we all must have played once in our childhood. In the BOLLYWOOD game, the participant has to guess a Bollywood movie which is presented in the form of missing letters. The amount of guesses are equal to the number of letters present in the word Bollywood. If the participant makes the correct guess within the provided number of guesses, he/she gets a point. Similarly, in the Chem-E-Wood game, the words related to Chemistry or Chemical Engineering were presented in the form of missing letters and the participants have to guess the right word. The amount of guesses should be equal to the number of letters in the word Chem-E-Wood. The winners were Students of 3rd year Chemical Engineering Rohan Faldu and Pratik Raja.

  • Date: 22nd January, 2020 (Wednesday)

Lockdown Buzzer

We are coming with the "Lockdown Buzzer". The game is designed in order to check knowledge of participants not only in the field of Chemical Engineering but also general knowledge, Aptitude and also some Non-Technical questions. The importance and uniqueness of the event will be the variety of questions asked. This would also strengthen our relations with each other, and a good amount of knowledge and information will flow from both sides.
Rules of the Game: A video or a picture will be shown on the Zoom meeting or Google meeting for a certain limit of time and a Questionnaire will be given in the form of GOOGLE FORM will be shared through WhatsApp group. The google form will accept answers for a limited time. The Judges will evaluate the answers and depending on the number of participants remaining, the top point scorer will be sent to the next round. The game will be of three rounds each with increased difficulty level and time limit for answering will also be decreased.
Round 1: Google form of 12 minutes Round 2: Google form of 10 minutes Round 3: Google form of 6 minutes

  • Date: 6th & 7th July, 2020
  • Time: 5 P.M onwards

Blast from the past

AIChE Ahmedabad university and AIChE NIT Rourkela is coming up with a event open for all "Blast from the Past".History has shown us plenty of disasters in chemical and nuclear plants, like Chernobyl and the Bhopal Gas Tragedy. Do you have what it takes to change history by going back in time and assuming a key role in a disaster?
Flow of the Event:- Participants register for the event One nuclear/ chemical disaster, and 3 key persons related to that disaster will be given to each participant Participants have to choose one key person, find info about them and write a write-up about what decisions they would change/ actions they would take to avoid or minimize the disaster. This write-up has to be submitted within 3 days of receiving their disaster and key persons.

  • Date: 25th July, 2020 (Saturday)

Chem-E-Jeopardy

Two members in a team are allowed. Questions will be read by the host. Once the host completes the question participants are requested to answer the question in the chat box. participant from whichever team answers correctly first gets the points for correct answer. The format of answer should be in type of question. (e.g. The answer is Rault’s Law; the format of answer should be “What is Raoult's Law”) Answers will be only considered after the host completes the question. If any answer given is wrong, the marks of that question will be deducted from your total score which your team was having before answering the question.
There will be different categories of questions related to chemical engineering and one section will be synonyms which will not be related to chemical engineering. (e.g. sports, politics, general knowledge etc.) Host’s call will be the final call. Scores will be announced after every round.

  • Date: 3rd October, 2020 (Saturday)
  • Time: 4:30 P.M
